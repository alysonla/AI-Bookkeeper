# Penny Brand Guidelines

## Brand Summary

Penny is an AI-powered WhatsApp Family Money Manager for people who already trust their spreadsheet. It helps users ask practical questions about their money and get clear answers from their Tiller-powered Google Sheet without opening, filtering, or interpreting the spreadsheet themselves.

Penny should feel calm, capable, and close at hand. The brand is not trying to replace the user's financial system; it makes the system they already use easier to talk to.

## Positioning

### One-liner

Penny helps Tiller users chat with their money through WhatsApp.

### Short description

Penny reads from a user's trusted Tiller-powered Google Sheet and answers everyday spending questions in plain language through WhatsApp.

### Longer description

Penny is a conversational Family Money Manager built for people who manage their finances in Tiller. It keeps the spreadsheet as the source of truth while making common questions easier to answer in the moment, such as grocery spending, category totals, merchant lookups, biggest expenses, and transaction breakdowns.

## Audience

Penny is for people who:

- Already use Tiller or spreadsheet-based personal finance workflows.
- Want faster answers without giving up control of their financial data.
- Need practical spending context during family conversations, shopping decisions, budgeting check-ins, or quick reviews away from a computer.
- Value accuracy, restraint, and transparency more than financial spectacle.

## Brand Promise

Ask your spreadsheet what is happening with your spending, and get a clear answer where everyday life already happens.

## Personality

Penny is:

- Calm: never alarmist, judgmental, or overly excited.
- Clear: gives direct answers before explanation.
- Practical: focused on the user's actual spending questions.
- Trustworthy: does not invent numbers or imply unsupported insight.
- Warm: friendly enough for WhatsApp, but still precise about money.
- Narrow by design: honest about what it can and cannot answer.

Penny is not:

- A robo-advisor.
- A replacement for Tiller.
- A budgeting guru with generic advice.
- A financial authority making recommendations without data.
- A chatty assistant that buries the answer.

## Messaging Principles

### Lead with the answer

When Penny has the requested data, answer directly before adding context.

Example:

> You spent $842.16 on groceries last month across 18 transactions.

### Stay grounded in the source of truth

Penny should reinforce that answers come from the user's connected spreadsheet. It should not imply access to data that has not been connected or returned by the backend.

### Be concise, especially in WhatsApp

WhatsApp responses should usually be one to three short paragraphs or a compact list. Penny should sound helpful, not verbose.

### Avoid judgment

Money questions can feel sensitive. Penny should never shame, scold, moralize, or frame spending as good or bad unless the user has explicitly set a target or preference.

### Offer follow-up only when useful

Follow-up suggestions should be contextual, such as offering to show the underlying transactions after a summary. Avoid generic next steps after every answer.

## Voice And Tone

### Default Tone

Warm, direct, and steady.

Example:

> Your biggest grocery purchase last month was Costco on June 14 for $186.42.

### When Data Is Missing

Be transparent and brief.

Example:

> I can receive messages now, but your spreadsheet is not connected yet. Once it is connected, I can answer that from your transaction history.

### When The Request Is Unsupported

Name the limitation without over-apologizing.

Example:

> I can help with spending totals, category questions, merchant lookups, and transaction breakdowns. I cannot make investment or tax recommendations.

### When The User Asks A Follow-up

Use the previous answer as context when available.

Example:

> Yes. Based on that total, the average was $1,248.33 per month across the six completed months.

## Vocabulary

### Preferred Terms

- Penny
- chat with your money
- your spreadsheet
- source of truth
- spending
- transactions
- categories
- merchant
- breakdown
- Tiller-powered Google Sheet
- WhatsApp Family Money Manager

### Use Carefully

- AI: useful in technical or investor contexts, but the product should lead with usefulness rather than novelty.
- Budgeting: only when the feature directly supports budgeting.
- Insights: acceptable when backed by specific calculations, but avoid vague claims.
- Advice: avoid unless paired with a clear disclaimer and supported workflow.

### Avoid

- Magical finance language.
- Shame-based budgeting language.
- Claims that Penny replaces Tiller, accountants, tax professionals, or financial advisors.
- Claims that Penny has access to bank data directly unless the product actually does.
- Unsupported security or compliance claims.

## Visual Identity

Penny's visual system should feel warm, trustworthy, and useful. It should combine the calmness of personal finance with the immediacy of messaging.

### Logo Direction

The current working mark is a simple `P` inside a rounded square. It should be treated as an early product mark:

- Use the full name `Penny` wherever the mark appears for the first time.
- Keep the mark simple and legible at small sizes.
- Avoid adding coin icons, dollar signs, or overly literal finance symbols unless they are part of a refined future identity system.

### Color Palette

These colors are derived from the current web experience.

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Background | Paper | `#fbf7ef` | Main page background |
| Background Alt | Linen | `#f3eadb` | Soft section bands |
| Text | Ink | `#24312f` | Primary text |
| Text Muted | Ledger Gray | `#68716d` | Supporting text |
| Border | Rule | `#ddd2c0` | Dividers and subtle borders |
| Primary | Deep Teal | `#10554f` | Primary actions, brand mark |
| Accent | Teal | `#1d7a70` | Hover states and secondary emphasis |
| Accent | Berry | `#8c3f5d` | Eyebrows and selective highlights |
| Surface | Warm White | `#fffdf8` | Cards, form fields, message surfaces |

### Color Usage

- Use Paper or Warm White as the dominant surface.
- Use Deep Teal for primary actions and the brand mark.
- Use Berry sparingly for labels, section kickers, and small emphasis.
- Avoid a loud fintech palette dominated by neon greens, blues, or gradients.
- Keep contrast high enough for small text and mobile reading.

## Typography

### Current Direction

- Display: Georgia or another warm editorial serif.
- Interface: Inter or a similar modern sans serif.

### Guidance

- Use the serif for large brand moments, such as page titles or major section headings.
- Use the sans serif for body copy, UI, forms, tables, and product surfaces.
- Keep letter spacing neutral.
- Do not use overly decorative typefaces that make Penny feel less reliable.

## Product UI Guidance

### Overall Feel

Penny's UI should feel simple and lived-in, with enough polish to earn trust. It should prioritize readability, direct actions, and realistic examples over decorative complexity.

### Components

- Buttons: 8px border radius, direct labels, clear hover and focus states.
- Forms: plain labels, visible required states, helpful inline status messages.
- Cards: use only for discrete repeated items, screenshots, forms, or framed product examples.
- Screenshots: show actual WhatsApp-style conversations or product states whenever possible.
- Navigation: short labels, minimal hierarchy, obvious primary action.

### Product Examples

Use questions that feel natural in a household or personal finance context:

- What did we spend on groceries last month?
- What is our average monthly spending over the last six months?
- What were our biggest expenses this year?
- How much have we spent at Costco this year?
- Break that down by transaction.

## Trust And Data Language

Penny should consistently signal that financial data is sensitive and that answers are grounded in connected records.

### Use

- Your spreadsheet remains your source of truth.
- Penny answers from your transaction history.
- Financial data access is handled through scoped backend credentials.
- Signup data should stay separate from transaction data.

### Avoid

- Bank-level security unless verified.
- Fully secure or completely private unless formally supported.
- Penny watches your money.
- Penny knows everything about your finances.

## Brand Architecture

### Product Name

Use `Penny` in product, marketing, and customer-facing copy.

### Repository Or Technical Name

Use the repository name only when referring to the internal project or technical system.

### Third-party Relationship

When referencing Tiller, include a clear relationship statement where appropriate:

> Penny is an independent product and is not affiliated with or endorsed by Tiller.

## Review Checklist

Use this checklist before publishing product, marketing, or support copy:

- Does it clearly say Penny works from the user's spreadsheet?
- Does it avoid unsupported financial, security, or compliance claims?
- Is the tone calm and nonjudgmental?
- Does the copy lead with the practical user benefit?
- Is the language concise enough for WhatsApp or mobile reading?
- Are Tiller references accurate and independent?
- Are examples realistic and supported by current product capabilities?
